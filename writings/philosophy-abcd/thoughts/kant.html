Kant. Kant kant kant. What am I even supposed to say?

Kant thinks he's being objective; that the rules he comes up, even if not being perfect, can be refined into optimal rules that are good for all of humanity. But his method of doing this, the Categorical Imperative, is just bad.
    To start with, the CI is based off of community values, which can change over time, and while values like "don't murder people" are pretty free and universal, laws like "slavery is bad" or valuing individual liberty are quite a bit less universal. Two different cultures could come to different conclusions on these issues, and both of them could be following the categorical imperative. So it can't be objective.

    Speaking of slavery. A perfect example of how flawed the categorical imperative is.
Kant supported slavery, because he thought that slaves weren't people. (I've been told.) So we can see how the categorical imperative can be used. While I could go "if everyone was a slave society would be horrible" and dismiss slavery, Kant goes "if everyone had slaves society would be cool actually, nothing wrong here" and because slaves aren't people, it's just as morally fine as owning cattle or spoons. Unless slavery is actually objectively good, the Categorical Imperative has failed in its goal of finding objective morality.
